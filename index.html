About

Hi, I'm Stephen. I'm an artist in the Boston area.
You can find my artwork for sale at Blue Cloud Gallery, Davis Squared, Magpie, and Porter Square Books.


Stephen Alajajian is a dedicated artist, writer, poet, activist, world traveler and nutritionist by trade. He takes inspiration from many places and from many people including BB King who once said, "I don't think anybody steals anything. All of us borrow." Stephen often encounters those who say they cannot decipher whether a work of art is good or bad. To these people, Stephen might say, "Are you feeling it? Does it speak to you?" If you are, and it does, then it's served its purpose. Regina Spektor once said, "You should only do art if you're inspired. There are plenty of other things to do in the world." Stephen once took a spur-of-the-moment trip to NYC in the midst of a scorching, sleepless and chaotic summer. "It was time in my life where I had all these creative and artistic ideas exploding out of me," he says about the time period. "And then I went down to New York for a weekend and just wandered through the Bronx playing the album 'Our Time in Eden', and to me, it felt like the whole city was on fire." At that time—and I will now stop talking about myself in the third person—at that time, I had an epiphany that there is in fact no need to create art— the depths of beauty in the manifested physical universe are unfathomably deep. Just look at biology. There couldn't be anything more incredible, more wondrous, more awe-provoking, than the complexity of the forest or the mechanisms by which cells and viruses replicate their DNA. Why create art? I think it's more for the artist, to once again see the world in a way that is new and refreshing. Sometimes as we get older we tend to see the world in ever more rigid ways. We can develop atherosclerosis of our own imaginations. If we can find ways to open up new worlds and light unto ourselves and to one another, I believe that we should. The collections here represent periods of inspiration and new perspective, and I hope they might take you somewhere cool.

I want to give a special thanks to some of the people who have inspired me the most in my life and in my artistic endeavors: Sharon Alajajian, Mike Abrams, Angelo Chandler, Damon Savage, Hayden Boska, Gonzalo Corts, Bhante Sujiva, Yasiin Bey, Marshall Matthers, Glenn Gould, John Mayer, Lynn Margulis, BB King, Bernie Sanders, Paul Pitchford, Michael Singer and so many more.

Named in honor of my good friends Jane Caporiccio and Amy Safier, check out my blog, Guardian Angels from San Francisco.
